What is PASS – Password Manager

PASS is a secure, offline-first password and credential manager designed to store your sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, bank details, API keys, and private notes in one place. Unlike cloud-based managers, PASS works directly in your browser using local storage, meaning your data stays on your device unless you explicitly export it.

The app is built for users who want simplicity, control, and privacy without depending on external servers or third-party data collection.

Why This App is Needed

Modern users manage dozens of accounts across social media, banking, work tools, and services. Reusing passwords or saving them in notes, screenshots, or browsers increases the risk of hacking, data leaks, and identity theft.

PASS solves this by providing a dedicated, structured, and secure place to store credentials, reducing human error and improving digital safety.

How PASS Protects Your Credentials

PASS uses your browser’s local storage to keep all credential data isolated on your device. No passwords are transmitted over the internet, uploaded to servers, or shared with third parties.

Each app entry is stored in a structured format with controlled access through the interface. Copy actions are manual, reducing accidental exposure. Long-press, text selection, and unauthorized copying are restricted to minimize data leakage.

Import and export functions are intentionally manual, ensuring that only you decide when and where your data is moved.

Offline & Local-Only Security Model

PASS operates fully offline after loading. This means it is immune to server breaches, cloud leaks, and remote attacks that commonly affect online password managers.

Since data never leaves your device automatically, the security responsibility stays in your hands — giving you maximum privacy and ownership.

Import & Export Safety

Exported data is saved as a structured file that you control. This allows safe backups, device migration, or offline storage such as encrypted drives.

After export, local data can be cleared to prevent unauthorized access, making PASS suitable for shared or temporary devices when used carefully.

Key Benefits of Using PASS

  • Works without internet after load
  • No cloud, no tracking, no ads
  • Full control over your data
  • Supports multiple account types (social, bank, API, custom)
  • Manual copy prevents accidental leaks
  • Lightweight, fast, and device-friendly

Who Should Use PASS

PASS is ideal for students, developers, freelancers, privacy-focused users, and anyone who wants a simple but powerful password manager without trusting external servers.

User Responsibility Notice

Since PASS stores data locally, users are responsible for device security, backups, and protecting exported files. Losing the device or storage without backup may result in permanent data loss.
